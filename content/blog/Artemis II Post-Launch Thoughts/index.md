It's been a couple hours now since launch, and the Artemis crew is now something like 5,000 miles above the Earth's surface!! I believe the ISS is only somewhere around 250 miles above the Earth, making the Artemis crew further than any human has gone in like 50 years (which was the Apollo 17 mission)! If nothing goes wrong, they should reach 250,000 miles from Earth in a few days!!

The Space Launch System (SLS), a pretty good name on the accuracy scale, is the most powerful vehicle created by NASA by many metrics. Rockets, by the way, are pretty much extraordinarily precisely controlled bombs that we put humans on top of in order to send them to space. This rocket is the biggest one NASA has made. Somehow, with the millions of things that can go wrong with bombs (and rockets), nothing is going wrong. Of course, things *can* go wrong, but things didn't go wrong, because some smart people figured out how to make a bomb go to the moon without things going wrong, which very very cool if you ask me.

## The Toilet Saga

The Orion spacecraft is probably the farthest humanity has sent a proper toilet to space, given that the Apollo missions didn't really have a *toilet* (they had plastic bags). Orion has an actual, honest to god, *toilet*, and quite the advanced toilet at that. The "Universal Waste Management System," commonly known as 'toilet,' is basically a little private room with a hatch/seat for solid waste, and a hose for liquid waste. The solid waste is stored and will be brought back to Earth, but the liquid waste is supposed to be... ejected into space. Lmao. Also, the toilet is so loud during operation that you need to wear hearing protection when using it lol{{ref(key="miller2026")}}

Unfortunately, the Universal Waste Management System, in all its groundbreaking space waste management glory, broke a couple hours into the mission. The crew could still poop, it's just the urination related functionality that broke.{{ref(key="malik2026")}}
